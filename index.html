Naive Bayes is, in Andrew Ng’s words, built on a strong assumption, where each of the words in a text is conditionally independent. Also, according to him, it is a practical learning algorithm. Although the numerical calculations may by someway difficult, the theorem itself is relatively easy, based on Bayes theory. Meanwhile, Andrew Ng introduced another model, which was based on multinomial distribution rather than multivariate gaussian one. This method behaved better in text detection, despite its neglect of the sequential factor.

Then he mentioned neural network, where the working mechnism was similar to logic regression, which was based on sigmoid function. And, in order to solve linearly inseperable problems, multilayer network was introduced, and also the back propagation algorithm, another name of gradient descent algorithm in a different condition. Then, it came to the distance in multidimensional problems, ending with the compare of functional and geometric margin.

To be honest, what I can do now may be only remember some terms without truely understanding them. Thus I need time to digest them. At the end, I want to state something I sadly find out today, which is the fact that maybe the real reason for my poor writing is not the low level in my English, on the other hand, it should be attributed to my inability of organizing words. I should do more writing everyday, both in Chinese and English.

3月24日的笔记

By Li

发表于 2015-03-24

I go on reading the book which introduces level set method, and mainly focus on the mean curvature flow and some other methods for building the velocity field. Admittedly, it seems not accessible for me, since I cannot even understand the mathmatic expressions. But I still hold on, and endeavor to figure out the method, which is so useful on the image processing area.

Simultaneously, I also pay attention to the linux system, and in process of understanding the virtual memory allocation. Compared with the hardware structure introduced in the theorem of computer class, it is more abstract. To manage the RAM more effectively and enhance the efficiency, the physical memory is artificially divided into lots of logical segment called pages. During the executing of process, the kernel puts the corresponding code into specific pages, building a excutable to record the relation between the code and pages. When needed, the kernel can directly find corresponding pages and execute the code, leaving the need for putting all of codes into the RAM unnecessary. As a result, with the same memory space, there will be more processes executed.

Then, when it comes to the command line arguments, things become easier, since I have already dealt with similar matters. Still, the details mentioned in the book such as how the string of arguments end greatly increase my knowledge scope. And the sections that talk about the environmental variables help me gain a clear idea of a question that has confused me for a long time, which is how the environmental variables work.

Today, while I have spent almost the whole day in the lab, I don’t regard what I obtain enough. In other words, I should learn more. 时间并不算很多，是时候来点鸡血了！

Partial differential equations are truly difficult for me, as well as the convection equation. I should work harder on them.

In order to get deep insight into operating system, I choose to study linux, yet the book mainly talks about the detail such as the number of the errors. Despite their importance, what I want to obtain is a systematical understanding. Hence I skip some pages and directly enter the part of process. After ernest reading, the definition of process is, in my opinion, an abstract entity defined by the kernel, which shares user-space and kernel resources and can execute the program’s code. Each process has a process ID, which, except from some irregularities such as init process, is randomly assigned by the kernel. And the creator of a process is called parent process. Once a process’s parent is orphaned, during its execution, it would be adopted by the init process. So far, I have known all above.

最后用中文感叹一下，刚才翻了一下高数下梯度的部分，居然在里面发现了与水平集方法中不少类似的定义。看来高数真的是工科理论知识无尽的瑰宝啊！以后多看。
